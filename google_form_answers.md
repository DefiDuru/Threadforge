## Page 2

6. What are you building?
ThreadForge is a multi-agent AI system that turns a raw project idea or
announcement into a complete, launch-ready X thread in minutes. It uses a
research agent powered by live web search to pull real stats and social
proof, then a writing agent that structures the thread around a proven
9-element framework: hook, relatable question, problem, solution flow, social
proof, human-readable structure, personal touch, practical use cases, and a
strong CTA. This means threads read like a real builder wrote them instead of
sounding like generic AI output. It's built for builders, founders, and
creators, including everyone in this HACD cohort, who need to build in public
but don't have the time or copywriting background to do it well. The goal is
a tool any project can plug their idea into and get back a thread that
actually drives engagement rather than reading like marketing copy.

9. Why does PoW / HACD make sense for your project?
HACD's principle of applying proof of work to assets maps naturally onto what
ThreadForge produces. Every thread it generates is the result of real,
verifiable agentic work, including live research, structured drafting, and
review, rather than a single lucky prompt. Issuing a Stack Token on HACD lets
early users and the builder community actually participate in a tool built
for this ecosystem's own growth, since nearly every cohort builder needs
launch threads at some point. That creates a direct feedback loop between the
asset and real utility. It also gives us a way to reward early adopters and
contributors with a real stake in the project as ThreadForge's capabilities
grow to cover more platforms, more frameworks, and team specific brand voice
training.

11. Initial idea for your Stack Token on HACD
The token, working name FORGE, would represent early access and ongoing
utility within ThreadForge. Holders would get priority access to new agent
capabilities as they roll out, such as additional platform support beyond X,
custom brand voice training, and higher usage limits on the research agent.
Distribution would be weighted toward early users who generate threads during
the incubator period and toward anyone in the HACD builder community who uses
ThreadForge to write their own launch content, since that is the most natural
and organic distribution channel available. Over time the token could also be
used to unlock collaborative features, like sharing or remixing thread
templates across the community, so usage and holding reinforce each other
rather than the token just being a passive collectible.
